Robotic Lunar Lander Project

Jeffrey Zhuang

Introduction

The goal of this project: produce a small drone that mimics the dynamics of a lunar lander, provides 60 seconds of flight time, is affordable, reusable, and will allow me to fly new and interesting guidance, navigation, and control systems.

I wrote vehicle physics simulations at 9 years old. After scrambling between interests of math, physics, and programming, I finally discovered the rocket landing problem and became intrigued. Since then, it is all I think about. I have written countless rocket landing simulations and taught myself optimal and predictive control, optimal state estimation, and optimal guidance along with the math.


However, flying it is the thing that makes you the subject matter expert; because you are able to perform analysis on performance, and drive requirements for the next time. The difference is flying it - the ultimate demonstration.
